Effects of Air Pollution

Air pollution has wide-ranging effects on both the environment and human health. Some key consequences include:

  • Respiratory Issues: Long-term exposure can lead to respiratory diseases like asthma and bronchitis.
  • Cardiovascular Problems: Increased risk of heart attacks and other cardiovascular issues.
  • Environmental Damage: Harm to plants, animals, and ecosystems; contributes to acid rain.
  • Climate Change: Greenhouse gases contribute to global warming and climate change.
  • Reduced Visibility: Particulate matter in the air reduces visibility, posing risks to transportation.
  • Impact on Wildlife: Wildlife health and habitats are affected, especially in urban areas.

It's crucial to address and mitigate air pollution for the well-being of both the planet and its inhabitants.
